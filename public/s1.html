Real Estate Agent Interview Process

Selling your home is a big deal — so while it may be tempting to go with the first word of mouth recommendation you get, when it comes to choosing a real estate agent, it pays to do a bit more research. Your real estate agent will be responsible for setting the sale price, marketing your home, running open houses, speaking with potential buyers and ultimately, walking you through the selling process. To make an informed choice, ask friends and neighbors for personal recommendations, but also take the time to interview several candidates before making a decision.





Which neighborhood is he/ she primarily works in?

If the agent typically works in areas far from your home, it may mean that he or she is not as familiar with the market in your area.

How will he/ she keep you informed about progress?

One of the biggest critisism about agents is a lack of communication. Find out how frequently you can expect your agent to check in with you, and when he or she is available for you to call with questions or for updates. Make sure your agent knows what you think is an appropriate level of communication.

Is this a full-time job?

Having another job shouldn’t necessarily cut a potential real estate agent out of the running, but you need to be aware if this is a part time gig before committing. Someone who also works somewhere else may be harder to reach and could miss out on opportunities to show your house.

How will you market my home?

There is a lot more to marketing a house than putting up for sale on the MLS. On how many websites will your agent list your home? Where will he or she look for buyers? A good marketing plan can be what makes the difference between a speedy sale and a home that languishes on the market.

What is the experience and education?

Though a new real estate agent can certainly be motivated and eager to please, and someone with years of experience will have the knowledge and skill to face unexpected challenges. Taking continuing education courses shows a commitment to keeping up with changes.

What are the negatives of my home?

You are not looking for a rainbows and unicorns response here. An agent that is a good fit should be able to name several potential drawbacks to selling your home, from repairs to location and beyond.

What type of support and resources does he/ she have?

This could include anything from technology help for creating a beautiful website for your home to an in house real estate attorney.

Do you just work with sellers, or do you represent buyers too?

There is nothing wrong with working with both as long as they don’t spread their time too thin. Buyers take up a lot of an agent’s time when showing homes.

To sum up:

It is common for real estate agents to prepare an informal presentation to give you in your home. This gives the agent a chance to see the property in person, and explain what he or she can do to represent your home. Consider keeping a written list of your questions in front of you during the presentation, and write down additional questions that occur to you during it. Wait until the end and ask any questions that were not covered.
