How to Choose a Real Estate Agent

Finding the right agent takes balancing credentials and chemistry. You want to choose someone you like after all; you might spend the next six months working together. But your agent also needs to be able to safeguard your financial interests.

You want somebody trustworthy who you can rely on. A good agent, who will listen carefully to your priorities and will not waste time on properties that do not fit the bill.

Here are some common ways buyers find agents:

Friends & Family: Maybe you have a friend or relative who happens to be a real estate agent. But just because you know the person well, does not mean she/ he would be the best agent for you. Does she/ he knows the neighborhoods where you want to buy? Is she/ he a tough negotiator? Will you be comfortable expressing your dissatisfaction, or even firing her if she/ he cannot get the job done? Hold a friend or relative to the same high standards you would expect from any agent.

Referral Friends and family who recently bought a home can be a good way to find your own agent. Make sure that the agent is familiar with your favorite areas.

Open Houses: Any time you sign a guest registry for an open house, you are putting yourself on the showing agent's call list. We do not recommend this method for ‘finding’ an agent, but if you do want to work with an agent you meet this way, do your homework (see Important Questions to Ask an Agent, below).

Internet: Many agents and brokerages have their own websites. In addition, review sites such as yelp.com can show you what other customers have to say about agents in your area.



Questions to Ask an Agent:


  1. The agent does real estate on the side, part time?
    Whether you are a buyer or seller, you want to choose an agent who is actively following the market every day. If you are buying, you want an agent who can jump on new listings and show them to you immediately. If you are the seller, you want an agent who is always available to show your home to prospective buyers.

  2. Look up the licensing
    Check with Miami Association of Realtors to find out if the person is licensed and if there have been any disciplinary actions or complaints, or check to see if the information is posted online.

  3. What's your fee?
    The seller pays the buyer's agent using the money you pay for the home, typically 3% of the sales price. Since the commission amount is set by the seller and can vary from home to home, you should insist that your agent is upfront about his share; you do not want an agent who pressures you into a home based on his chances of landing a fatter commission check.

  4. How quickly can you get me into a home?
    Hot homes move fast. Ask how the agent handles tours on short notice.

  5. What sets you apart from other agents?
    Look for expertise, not just enthusiasm. You want an agent with experience in your favorite neighborhoods, a proven track record of happy customers, and deep knowledge of any special requirements you might have in your home search.
