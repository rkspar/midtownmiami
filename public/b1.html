Financing Terms and Types

Financing can come in many forms and different lenders offer different variation of such terms.
Most Common Loan Types: Government and Conventional / Conforming and Non-Conforming
Government Loans are either FHA or VA mortgage loans, and are considered Non- Conventional. Home Equity Lines of Credit (HELOCs) are also considered Non-Conventional.





Government Loan Limits

  • (FHA Fair Housing Administration) varies by county, In Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach Counties the maximum FHA loan Limit is $345,000.
    Check you area here: https://entp.hud.gov/idapp/html/hicostlook.cfm
  • VA (Dept. of Veteran Affairs) follows conforming loan limits seen below

Conforming and Non-Conforming: Relates directly to the actual loan amount

  • In the state of Florida, and most other states, the conforming loan amount is $0 - $417,000 for 1-unit properties. See 2015-2016
    Loan limit confirmation letter here: https://www.fanniemae.com/content/announcement/ll1507.pdf
  • Non-Conforming loan amount is $417,001 and above 2+unit properties.

HELOC Loan Limits

  • There is no limit here and are almost always retained and serviced at the lender of origination.

FNMA or Freddie Mac almost always purchases both Government and Conventional Loans after the loan closes. Most major banks continue to service the loan by collecting payments, paying escrows, and in the case of default are responsible for collections and the foreclosure process.

Loan Terms:


FHA
  • Primary Residence Only
  • As little as 3.5% down or 96.5% LTV
  • 15 or 30 year fixed rates
  • Rates 2.5% and above, ARM 5 rates being the lower of the rates
  • 1,3,5,7, and 10 year fixed period with 30 or 15 year amortization ARMs available depending on the lender. Go to link for specific detail.
    http://portal.hud.gov/hudportal/HUD?src=/program_offices/housing/sfh/ins/203arm t
  • DO NOT have to be a first time home buyer
  • Requires both monthly mortgage insurance 1 (which is for the life of the loan) and upfront mortgage insurance. Go to link for specific detail.
    http://portal.hud.gov/hudportal/documents/huddoc?id=10-28ml.pdf
  • DTI of up to 50%
  • Financing approval and underwriting 6 is based on income, credit, assets and the overall ability to repay
VA
  • Primary Residence Only
  • Up to 100%LTV financing both purchase and refinance
  • 15 or 30 year fixed rates
  • 1,3,5,7, and 10 year fixed period with 30 or 15 year amortization ARMs available depending on the lender.
  • VA Funding Fee required
    http://www.benefits.va.gov/homeloans/documents/docs/funding_fee_table.pdf
  • The basic entitlement available to each eligible Veteran is $36,000. Lenders will generally loan up to 4 times a Veteran's available entitlement without a down payment, provided the Veteran is income and credit qualified and the property appraises for the asking price. For details visit:
    http://www.benefits.va.gov/homeloans/index.asp
    http://www.benefits.va.gov/homeloans/purchaseco_loan_limits.asp
  • Financing approval and underwriting is based on income, credit, assets and the overall ability to repay
Conventional Conforming
  • Up to 97% LTV financing (MI required above 80% for most FNMA loans), but 95% is more common for primary purchase transactions. See FNMA Eligibility Matrix for more info:
    https://www.fanniemae.com/content/eligibility_information/eligibility-matrix.pdf
  • 3, 5, 7, 10 year ARM (Adjustable Rate Mortgage) are most common:
    https://www.fanniemae.com/content/eligibility_information/arm-matrix.pdf
  • 10, 15, 20, 25, 30 year Fixed Rate Mortgages. Many lenders such as Citibank will allow for odd year amortization 2 such as 19 or 28 year amortization.
  • Rates 2.75% and above, ARM 5 rates being the lower of the rates, speak to your preferred lender for specific daily rates
  • Financing approval and underwriting is based on income, credit, assets and the overall ability to repay
Conventional Non-Conforming
  • Up to 90%LTV financing but most lenders do not go above 85%LTV, even though above 80% LTV no MI is required.
  • 3, 5, 7, 10 year ARM (Adjustable Rate Mortgage)
  • 10, 15, 20, 25, 30 year Fixed Rate Mortgages. Many lenders such as Citibank will allow for odd year amortization 2 such as 19 or 28 year amortization.
  • Typically for loan amounts $417,001 and above and almost what always held as a portfolio loan, meaning the bank doesn’t sell these loan and are kept in their book of business as a source of revenue
  • In some cases non-warrantable condos or loans better suited for non-conforming guidelines regardless of the loan amount will fall under this loan type
  • Financing approval and underwriting is based on income, credit, assets and the overall ability to repay
Reverse Mortgages
  • See Government Website:
    http://www.consumer.ftc.gov/articles/0192-reverse- mortgages
Hard Money, Private Equity, Creative Financing
  • LTV does not typically exceed 70% on homes and 60% on condos
  • Rates typically start at 7.5% or above but are more commonly 10% and above
  • Often require title be in a corporation name to avoid additional regulation/liability
  • Financing approval and underwriting is typically based on equity in the subject property and not credit and the ability to repay as it is with most traditional financing.
  • There are now lenders that lend in the gray area for those borrowers who don’t fit into either the traditional credit or income qualification bucket or the need for hard money. These loans typically go through an underwriting process similar to that of traditional underwriting but are less restrictive when it comes to credit requirements and DTI ratios.
Mortgage Insurance (MI or PMI)

-an insurance policy which compensates lenders or investors for losses due to the default of a mortgage loan. Mortgage insurance can be either public or private depending upon the insurer. Typically, necessary when LTV (loan-to- value) is greater than 80%. MI is often not required on lender portfolio products.

Amortization

The paying off of debt with a fixed repayment schedule in regular installments over a period of time.

LTV

Loan-to-Value or loan amount /the lesser of the purchase price or appraised value expressed as a percentage.

Example

  • Purchase Price = $100,000
  • Appraised Value = $105,000
  • Loan = $75,000
  • 75,000/100,000 = .75 or 75% LTV
CLTV = Combined Loan-To- Value
TLTV = Total Loan-To- Value
DTI

debt-to- income ratio, for more info on DTI front-end and back end Ratios

ARM – Adjustable Rate Mortgage
Underwriting

- the process in which everything contained I a loan application is validated through numerous resources including but not limited to credit bureaus, state and county property verification sites, the MLX, employers, landlords and banks.
