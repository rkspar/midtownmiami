How To Prepare Your Home For Showing

Are You Ready to Sell Your Home?


Many sellers don’t think that a little effort and time preparing for a scheduled showing can help tremendously in the sale of a home.  Not only can the correct preparation reduce the amount of time it takes for a home to sell, but also can lead to more money in the sellers pocket!

Don’t just list your home without any advance preparation. A few minor touch ups can go a long way towards making a favorable impression on potential buyers and perhaps insuring a deal.

Put your buyer’s hat on and walk thru your home like it is the first time and make notes on what you, as a buyer, would notice and then repair or replace those items.

Some of these recommendations are include getting rid of clutter, hiding family photos, organizing closets, removing excess or oversized furniture, and cleaning.


20 top ways to prepare your home for a sale


1. Clean, clean, clean Dust on top of the fan blades, polish your appliances and faucets, and give the windows a thorough washing. If you have already moved out or if you are too busy to stay on top of these things, you should consider hiring a professional cleaning service that could stop by every couple of weeks.

2. Pay attention to smells. Do not cook bacon in your home the day of a showing, although it might taste great, but the smell is strong and lingers for a long time. You do not want your home to smell like a fast food restaurant.



3. Empty garbage cans. All garbage cans need to be empty. Also wash them out once a week and give them a quick spay of disinfectant to cut down odors.

4. Clear out the clutter. You want buyers to focus on how awesome your space is, not how messy it looks. Some areas that often collect clutter that should be checked are stairs, entryways, and closets. Put away that pile of shoes from the entry, that stack of mail from the kitchen table and anything else that reduces from your home’s gorgeous features.



5. Repaint the walls to neutral colors. As much as you love your dramatic red dining room, it could turn off a good portion of your buyers. Repaint your rooms in neutral tones like tans and whites that allow buyers to focus on the spaces, not the color of the walls.



6. Keep the décor simple. To help buyers imagine themselves in your space, get rid of any art or other décor that might turn off people with different tastes. A classic landscape painting is totally fine. Your zebra print leather couch? Might want to slipcover that for showings.

7. Get rid of personal items. Buyers want to be able to envision themselves in your home, so remove anything overly personal, like family photos in the hallway or your kid’s artwork on the fridge.



8. Let there be light! Open up all the windows, blinds and drapes to let in natural light and add floor or table lamps to areas that are dim. A bright, cheery room looks bigger and more inviting.

9. Bring nature inside. Few pretty buds in a vase can help bring energy into a space, fill in empty corners and even draw attention to features you want buyers to notice. Just make sure the flowers are in good health (and bug-free!).



10. Check the Windows. Drapes and blinds are often a part of a home that is neglected for general cleaning and maintenance.  Make sure the blinds and drapes are clean, but also that they are functioning.  A buyer is often concerned with the quality of the windows in a potential home and will be inspecting them so make all the windows are clean and sparkling! Finally, if weather allows, crack a window to allow some nice refreshing air to flow through the home.



11. Get rid of bulky furniture. Your furniture should fit the scale of the room, so get rid of any extra or oversized items that could make your space look smaller than it really is.

12. Organize your closets. Storage space is a huge selling point, and if your closets are stuffed to the brim, buyers will think you don’t have enough of it. Invest in some boxes, dividers and other solutions that will help you make your stuff look more organized, and remove extra items you don’t need immediately.



13. Grab that honey-do list. All those little things you’ve been meaning to do, but never got around to... Buyers will notice them, and the will detract from the value of your home. So set aside a weekend to tighten those loose doorknobs, fix that leaky faucet and paint over the scuffs from when you first moved in your sofa.



14. Turn the living room into conversation central. Help buyers picture themselves relaxing with family and guests by regrouping your furniture into arrangements that inspire conversation.

15. Do a faux “renovation. Little tweaks can make a big difference in the overall feel of a room. If the kitchen is a little outdated replace the fixtures, faucets and hinges. If in the living room the furniture beaten up then throw some slipcovers over it.



16. Turn the bathroom into a spa. Create the feel of a relaxing, luxurious bath — for less than $30. Stack a few pretty washcloths tied with ribbon, add some scented candles and faux plants, and buy bathmats and towels in coordinating tones such as light green, blue and white. Bathrooms are often times the second biggest selling feature of homes. Is the vanity top clean and clear of clutter?  Is the floor clean?  Another area to look at before a showing is the bath or shower.  Is this tub or shower clean?  Most prospective buyers are going to look inside the tub and/or shower and a dirty tub and/or shower are not pleasant. A clean bathroom appearance can go a long way with a potential buyer.



17. Close the toilet! When it comes to both showing and photographing your home, this little trick can make a surprising difference.



18. Check the Floors. From the kitchen to the bathroom floors, all floors throughout a home should be checked before a showing.  If there are carpeted rooms, are they vacuumed and free from stains? Replacing and/or cleaning of damaged/dirty carpet is a quick, easy, and cheap home selling tip that should be strongly considered.



19. Make something yummy. Realtors don’t put out fresh cookies at open houses just to treat buyers; a “homey” smell like baking cookies or bread can help people connect with a kitchen. Not a baker? Fake it with a scented candle.



20. Check the Temperature. This applies for any home located anywhere.  Depending on the time of the year, make sure your homes temperature is comfortable.  While it’s hot outside, set the thermostat between 70-74 degrees.  You want the buyer to feel instant relief from the heat once they enter your home.
