Physical Inspection (What it consist of)

Floors


  • The floor is the first you notice in an apartment
  • Make sure the carpets are cleaned and without snags or tears
  • The carpets should not be stained and should not smell
  • Check the kitchen and bathroom floors if they have been mopped and swept
  • The tiles should not be missing, scraped, scuffed or popping out

Walls


  • Check the paint on the walls, it should be consistent throughout the apartment and not be chipped
  • Make sure there are no stains on the walls
  • Check the baseboards if they are not damaged either

Windows


  • Every room should have at least one window that can be opened
  • You face a huge safety hazard if some windows do not open properly
  • Make sure the windows are not leaking and the glass is not chipped or broken
  • The windows should be able to lock from the inside
  • The glass should be clean, free of mold and mildew
  • Check the blinds make sure they are not dirty or stained
  • They should work properly and not be broken

Electricity


  • Flick all of the light swithes in the apartment, see if they work
  • Make sure none of them has damage or have loose wires hanging out
  • Each switch should have a faceplate as well
  • If the lights do not go on, check the bulbs. Usually, they are burned out
  • The lights should not flicker or throw sparks either
  • Plug a small appliance like a hair dryer into each of the outlets to make sure they all work
  • Check the fuse box it should not be damaged or have missing switches

HVAC


Now it is time to check out the apartment heating and air conditioning unit, also called the HVAC unit.

  • Make sure the thermostat works and shows the accurate temperature in the apartment
  • The unit should be able to blow both hot and cold air. Make sure it does.
  • Check if there are any loose wires or hoses sticking out of the unit
  • It should not be leaking water
  • Check the filter if it is not clogged or dirty
  • Change the filter every month even if it is just a little bit dirty because it can cause the HVAC unit to run inefficiently and drive your energy bill up

Fire Safety


Smoke detectors are very important to your safety when living in an apartment. Fire can spread quickly through and other shared areas in the complex, so the smoke detectors are often your best bet to protect yourself.

  • Should be a smoke alarm in every bedroom
  • The kitchen should have one as well
  • Check all smoke alarms are working properly
  • Make sure they have fresh batteries in them
  • Be sure and test them every month
  • The unit should have a carbon monoxide detector installed
  • See if a working fire extinguisher somewhere in near the apartment

Bathrooms


  • Turn on the shower in the bathroom
  • See if the water is clean and it gets hot and cold
  • Check the shower walls for any missing or damaged tiles and for mold
  • See if the toilets are clean, flush properly and do not leak
  • Look if they are not running when not in use
  • Check the sinks if the faucet runs hot and cold here as well, and the water drains properly
  • Note any damage on the faucets
  • Look into the cabinets under the sink and note any leaky water, mold or strange smell

Bedrooms & Doors


Now let's take a look at all the doors in the apartment:

  • All doors should shut tightly and fit properly in the frame
  • Should not be any problems with the doorknobs or the locks
  • See if any paint is missing, mismatched or chipped
  • The front door should have a deadbolt lock
  • Check the closets and make sure the shelves are not loose

Kitchen


Most stoves and ovens are electric (rather than gas) in apartment complexes.

  • Check the switches if they all work property
  • Test the burners when you turn them on to get hot
  • Should not be any frayed wiring around the oven
  • If the unit has a gas stove make sure it does ignite properly
  • Make sure you do not smell gas when it is in use
  • Check the carbon monoxide detector if working well
  • Look in the refrigerator if it is clean, cold and there is no mold in it
  • Check inside the kitchen cabinets for any mold or mildew
  • Look for animal droppings that can indicate a pest problem

Pests


Now let's get to the gross part of apartment inspection. It is time to check around for insect and rodent droppings. It may be a bit unsettling, but it is necessary.

  • Seach around the apartment with a flashlight in areas where pests are known to enter or gather
  • Look at gaps or cracks in walls and ceilings, near the tub and plumbing, around utility wires, behind the stove and in the kitchen cabinets
  • Search for small brown pellets or insect eggs
  • Look for any sign of roaches or mice
  • Note any of these listed issues becuase it's time for some pest control
